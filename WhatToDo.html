Local Parties

  • January 16: Bonfires of San Antón are celebrated on the night of January 16, bonfires are made in several neighborhoods of the town where people dance, tasting the typical sausages and "corn flowers".
  • February 2: Candlemas, popularly known as Our Lady of the threads. They make bread and oil sweets in a circular and flattened shape and they are blessed, later they are tasted in the city for the celebration.
  • March 19: Divina Pastora. The Brotherhood of the Divine Shepherdess or of the Shepherds is very old, being able to be traced documentarily to the XVIII century. Apparently it was founded by a pastor from Jaén. It is traditional not to receive public worship in any church, only in the home of the graceful pastor in the draw of September 8 to have six months, this grace is called "Piostre".
  • May:
    • Festivities Spring in honor of the Virgin of Fatima. During the days 13, 14 and 15 of May the festivities of the neighborhood of Fatima are celebrated with popular festivals in the Plaza de Fátima. On the 13th, one of the most emotional moments of the town takes place, when Our Lady of Fatima walks the streets of her neighborhood and other surrounding neighborhoods, taking a different route every year.
    • Pilgrimage San Isidro Labrador. It takes place on May 15, makes its departure from the Church-Sanctuary of the Most Holy Christ of Mercy to the place known as Garcíez Fountain, where his hermitage is located. During the journey, he is accompanied by horsemen and cazorras.
  • May - June:
    • Corpus. The Corpus Christi party goes through the streets decorated with colorful popular altars, during the odd years it leaves from the Parroquia de la Asunción and even years from the parish of Nuestra Señora de Fátima. The custody is of 1669.
    • Pilgrimage Virgen de la Cabeza. It is celebrated at the end of the month of May or the beginning of the month of June. Take a tour through the streets of the town.
  • September:
    • From September 1 to September 5 the Real Feria de Septiembre is celebrated, founded by royal decree of Isabel II in 1837, which stands out for its bullfights and its lively fairs. The cover opens on August 31.
    • September 7. Divina Pastora. The procession is the most striking religious act of this peculiar celebration that reflects the dedication with which the pastors celebrate their patron. they decorate the streets through which their patron passes with green arches, flowered pots and handmade quilts on the balconies.
    • September 13 and 14. The second party takes place in honor of the Most Holy Christ of Mercy, which returns to procession through the city.
  • September-October: Our Lady of the Rosary and the Aurora. A devotion that goes back to the 16th century that was recovered in 2008. Walk through the streets of the town in procession from the Church-Sanctuary of the Most Holy Christ of Mercy.
  • November: The Saints. The party takes place on November 1, when the door locks with porridge are covered, to avoid the death spirits entering.
  • December: Munidas de Santa Lucía, a tradition lost and recovered for a few years and where the neighbors make a tour of different streets of the town singing Christmas carols, ending with the tasting of typical Christmas sweets.

Easter

 Considered one of the best in the province of Jaén, for the quality of its sizes, steps and equipment of famous artists and goldsmiths. Highlights events such as the Veronica and the meeting at dawn of Good Friday of the Paso de Jesús Nazareno and Paso de María Santísima de la Amargura; the climb to the "race" of the Cuesta de San Femando of the Palio de María Santísima de Caridad y Piedad, after the Mystery Pass of La Columna; the difficult exit of the Mystery Pass of the Brotherhood of the Christ of Expiration through the door of La Asunción and the Palio de María Santísima del Calvario; the seriousness of the procession of the Holy Burial by the Plaza de España in the darkness between the singing of arrows ... the Step of Mystery of the Prayer of the Garden of great slenderness work of Palma Burgos and the elegance and majesty of the Palio of Our Lady of Hope for the slope of San Fernando street, to the song of La Salve; the solemnity of the Step of the Christ of Humility, and the refinement of the Palio of Our Lady of Faith and Love ... the distinctive procession of the Risen One; the Step of the Entrance of Jesus in Jerusalem carried by boys and girls; or the silence and austerity of the Via Crucis del Cristo del Consuelo y la Merced, describe the most exciting moments that are experienced during this week.
